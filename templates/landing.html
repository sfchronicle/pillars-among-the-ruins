During the height of the AIDS crisis in San Francisco, about 30 people a week died from the virus. All told, AIDS has claimed 20,000 lives in San Francisco. The loss to society of so much promise can never be calculated. But AIDS didn’t just kill, it maimed the futures of so many survivors who came of age under a death sentence.

Now, 20 years after HIV became a treatable, survivable disease, and as the city and country push aggressive campaigns to stop all new infections and eradicate AIDS, the survivors wonder what becomes of them. No one expected survivors. No one planned for their futures. There’s no one to care for them and they feel abandoned by their city and their community.
